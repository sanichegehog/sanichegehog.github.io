An analytical examination on the history of memes

The word "meme" originated with scientist Richard Dawkins in his book "The Selfish Gene". Evolution, according to Dawkins, did not depend on the specific chemical basis of genetics, but only on the existence of a self-replicating unit of transmission — in the case of biological evolution, the gene. A meme is a sort of parellel to this replication of genes. The exact definition of a meme is "an idea, behavior, or style that spreads from person to person within a culture".

Internet Memes

The first major internet meme was Greenoch, the original flamewar. The meme, created in 1991 by Doug Welch, and was a fictional Celtic martial arts. Many believed him, and a debate ensued to see who understood the true “Greenoch” Soon, it was described in detail, with styles, weapons, and the whole 9 yards fought over and debated. Nobody won of course, but it had its place as the first flame war in internet history.

The Spirit of Christmas

Without this meme, there would be no South Park

Fox executive Brian Graden initially distributed the video to 80 friends in December 1995. The video got passed around and eventually got into the hands of Comedy Central who loved the short so much, they decided to make a show out of it.

Hamster Dance

The Hamster Dance was created originally as a contest between two Canadian art students on who could generate the most web traffic. It was up for about a year and a half and received little attention. Then in January 1999, with no warning, the site got ~15,000 views per day and quickly became a meme.

Peanut butter jelly time

Peanut Butter Jelly Time is a Flash animation that emerged in the late 1990s and became an Internet phenomenon. Based on a song of the same name recorded by the Buckwheat Boyz, it features a repetitive chorus accompanied by a dancing banana.

Numa Numa

The Numa Numa video, was an internet phenomenon that featured an adolecent boy, Gary Brolsma, dancing to a Moldavian pop song. This video became an instant hit and even got Brolsma featured on Good Morning America and The Tonight Show . This video was so popular, a contest was created for the best parody, promising 45,000 dollars to the winner.

The Maze Game

"The Maze Game" is a game where users try to navigate a dot through 4 simple mazes with their mouse until the possessed face of Regan MacNeil, the principal character of The Exorcist, appears with a high-pitched scream during a point when players are concentrating intently at a tricky part of the game. It gained popularity in late 2005.
